## Artist statement

I am a Minneapolis based creative coder, musician, and moving image artist. My work combines guitar, live visual processing, webcam footage, screen recordings, creative coding, digital distortion, and analog printmaking. I am interested in the laptop as an instrument, the screen as a performance space, and technology as a portal for Black sound, memory, spirituality, and futurity.

The work in this portfolio sits between experimental music video, visual art, performance documentation, live coding, and digital collage. Across the projects, I return to saturated color, layered screens, feedback, imperfect edges, handmade abstraction, and the relationship between analog and computational image making.

## Featured Project: risograph supermarketsallad

Experimental moving image / live coded visual performance / guitar study.

risograph supermarketsallad is an experimental moving image piece combining guitar performance, live visual processing, screen recording, webcam footage, and saturated digital distortion. The work treats the laptop screen as a performance space, where music, body, code, and image collapse into one layered transmission.

The piece connects to Afrofuturism through its use of technology as atmosphere and portal. Rather than telling a traditional story, it creates a Black digital interior: a space of color, feedback, rhythm, memory, and spiritual noise.

## Haystack Printmaking Experience

Through a scholarship supported class at Haystack, I studied introductory printmaking and lithography. I created cut paper forms with an X Acto knife and translated them into printed compositions, exploring color, layering, abstraction, and handmade image construction. This experience informs my digital moving image work, especially my interest in saturated color, texture, distortion, and the relationship between analog and computational image making.
